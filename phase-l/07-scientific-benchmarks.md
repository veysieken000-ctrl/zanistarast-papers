# Scientific Benchmark Framework

Version: 1.0 Draft

Status: Scientific Validation

---

# Purpose

This document establishes the Scientific Benchmark Framework of the Zanistarast Scientific Synthesis.

Scientific benchmarks provide objective, reproducible and measurable evaluation of scientific methods, mathematical models, computational systems and knowledge structures.

Benchmarking supports continuous scientific improvement through deterministic comparison.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Every scientific model shall be evaluated using measurable criteria.

Scientific comparison shall rely on reproducible evidence rather than authority or opinion.

# Benchmark Categories

The Scientific Benchmark Framework evaluates

• scientific theories,

• mathematical models,

• computational algorithms,

• artificial intelligence systems,

• engineering systems,

• social models,

• historical analyses,

• interdisciplinary systems.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

Every benchmark shall define measurable indicators including

• accuracy,

• reproducibility,

• consistency,

• explanatory capability,

• predictive capability,

• computational efficiency,

• scalability,

• verification completeness.

Additional criteria may be introduced when appropriate for a specific scientific domain.

---

# Benchmark Dataset

Every benchmark requires

• documented input,

• documented methodology,

• reproducible execution,

• measurable output,

• certified verification.

---

# Comparative Analysis

Benchmark comparison shall identify

• strengths,

• limitations,

• uncertainty,

• reproducibility,

• opportunities for improvement.

Comparisons shall focus on methods and results rather than individuals or institutions.

---

# Rasterast Certification

A benchmark becomes certified only after

• successful execution,

• complete documentation,

• reproducibility,

• verification consistency,

• Rasterast Certification.

---

# Scientific Improvement

Benchmarking is not intended to establish permanent superiority.

Its purpose is to identify opportunities for improving scientific understanding and methodology through verified evidence.

Future versions shall define

• benchmark libraries,

• benchmark certification,

• benchmark repositories,

• benchmark statistics.
