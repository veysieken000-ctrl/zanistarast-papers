Version: 1.0 Draft

Status: Scientific Validation

# Purpose

This document establishes the Experimental Book of the Zanistarast Scientific Synthesis.

The Experimental Book defines how every scientific claim shall be verified through documented, reproducible and auditable scientific investigation.

Its objective is to transform scientific ideas into verifiable knowledge.

---

# Fundamental Principle

A scientific claim becomes stronger only through successful verification.

Every experiment shall be reproducible.

Every result shall remain open to future verification.

# Types of Experiments

The Experimental Book supports

• laboratory experiments,

• computational experiments,

# Experiment Structure

Every experiment shall contain

• Experiment Identifier

• Scientific Question

• Objective

• Initial Hypothesis

• Required Data

• Methodology

• Observations

• Results

• Verification

• Conclusion

# Evidence

Evidence shall include

• observations,

• measurements,

• documents,

• simulations,

• historical records,

• mathematical proofs,

• computational outputs,

• reproducible datasets.

Evidence shall always be traceable.

---

# Experiment Classification

Every experiment receives one status

• Planned

• Running

• Completed

• Verified

• Requires Revision

• Rejected

Only verified experiments contribute to certified scientific knowledge.

---

# Documentation

Every experiment permanently records

• identifier,

• version,

• author,

• date,

• verification history,

• certification status.

Documentation remains immutable after certification.

Experimental knowledge is cumulative.

Verified experiments strengthen scientific understanding.

Unverified experiments become opportunities for future investigation.
