# Sources of Scientific Evidence

Scientific evidence may originate from

• laboratory experiments,

• historical events,

• natural systems,

• biological systems,

• geological systems,

• astronomical observations,

• computational simulations,

• mathematical proofs,

• engineering implementations,

• reproducible social observations.

Each source shall be evaluated according to its own verification methodology.

---

# Multi-Layer Investigation

Every phenomenon shall be investigated through multiple complementary layers.

Possible investigation layers include

• physical,

• biological,

• psychological,

• sociological,

• ecological,

• geological,

• chronological,

• informational,

• mathematical,

• ethical.

The applicable layers depend on the nature of the phenomenon under study.

---

# Scientific Integration Principle

Verified knowledge shall never remain isolated.

Every verified discovery is connected with existing verified knowledge through the Knowledge Graph and evaluated by the Rasterast Verification Filter.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Scientific knowledge is continuously refined.

New evidence may

• strengthen,

• extend,

• reorganize,

or replace previous models when supported by stronger verification.

---

# Universal Knowledge Principle

Verified knowledge has no permanent owner.

Knowledge that passes the Rasterast Verification Filter and is compatible with Hebûn and Zanabûn becomes part of the Zanistarast Scientific Synthesis regardless of where, when or by whom it was discovered.

---

# Scientific Responsibility

Researchers shall seek

• truth before opinion,

• verification before acceptance,

• evidence before certainty,

• consistency before publication.
