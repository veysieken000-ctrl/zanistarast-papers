# Peer-Reviewed Scientific Publication Framework

Version: 1.0 Draft

Status: Scientific Validation

---

# Purpose

This document defines the scientific publication framework of the Zanistarast Scientific Synthesis.

Scientific publications communicate certified knowledge to the scientific community in a structured, reproducible and verifiable form.

Every published result shall remain open to independent examination and future refinement.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Publication is not the completion of scientific investigation.

Publication is the beginning of broader scientific verification.

Scientific knowledge becomes stronger through independent examination and reproducible verification.

# Publication Categories

The framework supports

• theoretical papers,

• mathematical papers,

• computational papers,

• engineering papers,

• historical investigations,

• interdisciplinary studies,

• benchmark reports,

• experimental reports.

---

# Scientific Paper Structure

Every publication shall contain

• Title

• Abstract

• Motivation

• Scientific Question

• Definitions

• Methodology

• Evidence

• Results

• Discussion

• Limitations

• Future Work

• References

---

# Verification Requirements

Every publication shall provide

• reproducible methodology,

• documented evidence,

• traceable references,

• verification history,

• certification status.

---

# Independent Examination

Scientific publications encourage

• replication,

• constructive criticism,

• alternative analysis,

• independent verification,

• scientific improvement.

Independent examination strengthens scientific knowledge.

---

# Scientific Integrity

Every publication shall distinguish between

• verified results,

• hypotheses,

• interpretations,

• open questions,

• future investigations.

---

# Certification

A scientific publication may receive

• Draft

• Under Review

• Verified

• Certified

• Revised

• Archived

Only Certified publications become part of the Certified Scientific Library.
