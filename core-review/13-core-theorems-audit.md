# Core Theorems Audit

Version: 1.0

Status: Core Review

---

# Purpose

This document audits every certified theorem within the Zanistarast framework.

While axioms establish the foundational rules,

theorems demonstrate the deterministic consequences of those rules.

Every certified theorem shall be traceable to the primitive definitions and certified core axioms.

---

# Scope

The audit evaluates

• theorem validity,

• dependency correctness,

• proof completeness,

• deterministic derivation,

• compatibility with Rasterast verification.

---

# Theorem Requirements

Every certified theorem shall satisfy

• derivability from certified axioms,

• deterministic proof,

• absence of hidden assumptions,

• compatibility with the primitive dependency chain,

• mathematical consistency.

A theorem failing any requirement shall be revised or withdrawn from the certified core.

---

# Dependency Structure

Every theorem shall follow the dependency order

Primitive Definitions

↓

Primitive Chain

↓

Core Axioms

↓

Certified Theorems

No theorem may introduce a new primitive concept or modify an existing certified axiom.

---

# Proof Traceability

Each theorem shall include a complete proof trace.

The proof shall identify

• primitive definitions used,

• axioms applied,

• intermediate derivation steps,

• final conclusion.

No unexplained inference shall remain.

---

# Consistency Analysis

Each theorem is evaluated for

• logical consistency,

• mathematical consistency,

• dependency preservation,

• compatibility with previous certified theorems.

Conflicting theorems cannot simultaneously remain certified.

---

# Rasterast Verification

A theorem is considered Rasterast compatible only if

• every dependency is certified,

• every inference is deterministic,

• every conclusion is reproducible,

• no contradiction exists within the dependency chain.

---

# Audit Output

The audit produces

• Certified Theorem List,

• Proof Dependency Matrix,

• Consistency Report,

• Revision Recommendations.
