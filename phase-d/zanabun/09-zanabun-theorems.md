# Theorem 1

Knowledge Consistency Theorem

Statement

Every certified Zanabûn Element preserves semantic consistency under every certified Zanabûn Operator.

Consequence

Certified knowledge cannot become internally contradictory through certified reasoning.

---

# Theorem 2

Semantic Preservation Theorem

Statement

Every certified semantic transformation preserves the deterministic meaning of the transformed knowledge.

Consequence

Representation may change without changing certified meaning.

---

# Theorem 3

Explainability Theorem

Statement

Every certified knowledge element possesses at least one complete deterministic explanation.

Consequence

Certified reasoning always remains reconstructable.

---

# Theorem 4

Dependency Integrity Theorem

Statement

Every certified inference preserves the complete dependency chain of every participating knowledge element.

Consequence

No certified inference destroys knowledge provenance.

---

# Theorem 5

Certified Inference Theorem

Statement

Every certified inference generated from certified premises is itself eligible for certification.

Consequence

Certified reasoning is recursively extensible.

---

# Theorem 6

Knowledge Closure Theorem

Statement

The application of any certified Zanabûn Operator to certified knowledge produces another certified knowledge element.

Consequence

Certified reasoning remains inside Zanabûn Space.

---

# Theorem 7

Deterministic Learning Theorem

Statement

Given identical certified knowledge, identical learning operators always generate identical certified knowledge.

Consequence

Learning remains reproducible.
