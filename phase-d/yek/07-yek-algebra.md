Definition 1

(𝕐, Ω)

# Closure Law

For every

y₁,y₂ ∈ 𝕐

every certified operator

⊗

shall satisfy

y₁ ⊗ y₂ ∈ 𝕐

only if

• uniqueness is preserved,

• dependency is preserved,

• certification remains valid.

---

# Identity Law

There exists an identity operator

I

such that

I(y)=y

for every

y ∈ 𝕐.

Identity shall never be modified by certified algebraic operations.

---

# Composition Law

Operator composition

Ω₂ ∘ Ω₁

is certified only when

• both operators are certified,

• uniqueness is preserved,

• dependency integrity remains valid,

• Rasterast verification succeeds.
