# Chapter 05 — Independence of Axioms

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This chapter evaluates whether the core axioms introduced in Book I are logically independent.

An axiom is independent if it cannot be derived from the remaining axioms.

The objective is to identify unnecessary or redundant assumptions.

---

# Independence Criteria

An axiom shall satisfy all of the following conditions.

---

## Criterion 1 — Necessity

Removing the axiom changes the mathematical framework.

Status

Pending

---

## Criterion 2 — Independence

The axiom cannot be derived from the remaining axioms.

Status

Pending

---

## Criterion 3 — Consistency

The axiom does not introduce contradiction.

Status

Pending

---

## Criterion 4 — Productivity

The axiom contributes to new lemmas or theorems.

Status

Pending

---

# Validation Procedure

For every core axiom

Step 1

Temporarily remove the axiom.

Step 2

Attempt to derive the same mathematical results.

Step 3

If every result is still derivable,

the axiom is redundant.

Otherwise,

the axiom is independent.

---

# Scientific Review Note

The objective is not to maximize the number of axioms.

The objective is to identify the smallest set of axioms sufficient to generate the mathematical structure of Book I.
