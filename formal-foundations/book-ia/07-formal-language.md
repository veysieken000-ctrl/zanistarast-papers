# Chapter 07 — Formal Language

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This chapter defines the formal language used to express the Zanistarast mathematical framework.

The language specifies symbols, formulas and admissible expressions.

It introduces no new mathematical concepts.

---

# Alphabet

The language consists of

- primitive symbols,
- variables,
- function symbols,
- relation symbols,
- logical symbols,
- punctuation symbols.

# Well-Formed Formula

A statement is well formed if it satisfies the syntactic rules of the language.

Ill-formed expressions are not admissible.

---

# Formation Rules

Functions may only be applied to objects belonging to their domains.

Relations may only compare compatible objects.

Every theorem must be expressible as a well-formed formula.
