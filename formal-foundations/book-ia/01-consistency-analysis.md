# Chapter 01 — Consistency Analysis

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This chapter evaluates the internal consistency of the mathematical foundations established in Book I.

No new definitions, axioms or mathematical objects are introduced.

---

# Validation Levels

The consistency analysis is divided into five independent levels.

---

## Level 1 — Definition Consistency

Objective

Verify that every primitive concept has exactly one definition throughout Book I.

Validation Questions

- Does each primitive concept have a unique meaning?
- Are there conflicting interpretations?
- Are later chapters compatible with the original definitions?

Pending

---

## Level 2 — Notation Consistency

Objective

Verify that mathematical notation is used consistently.

Validation Questions

- Does every symbol have exactly one meaning?
- Are mappings used consistently?
- Are domains and codomains preserved?

Status

Pending

---

## Level 3 — Axiom Consistency

Objective

Determine whether the core axioms can coexist without contradiction.

Validation Questions

- Can two axioms produce incompatible conclusions?
- Are all axioms mutually compatible?
- Is any axiom redundant?

Status

Pending

---

## Level 4 — Structural Consistency

Objective

Verify that the deterministic architecture preserves the intended dependency structure.

Validation Questions

- Does every layer depend only on previous layers?
- Can any layer be bypassed?
- Is the deterministic chain structurally coherent?

Status

Pending

---

## Level 5 — Derivation Consistency

Objective

Verify that every lemma and theorem is derivable from previous results.

Validation Questions

- Does each theorem cite its dependencies?
- Are hidden assumptions introduced?
- Is the dependency graph acyclic?

Status

Pending

# Scientific Review Note

Consistency analysis evaluates the internal coherence of the framework.

It does not establish correctness with respect to the external world.

External applicability requires additional mathematical, computational and empirical investigation.
