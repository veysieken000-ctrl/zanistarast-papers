# Primitive Definitions

Version: 1.0

Status: Core Definitions

---

# Purpose

Primitive definitions are the irreducible concepts of Zanistarast Mathematics.

They are accepted as foundational objects from which every higher mathematical structure is constructed.

Primitive definitions are intentionally left undefined in terms of simpler concepts.

Instead, they serve as the starting point of the entire mathematical framework.

---

# Primitive Object 1

# Ehad

Ehad denotes the Absolute Unity.

Within Zanistarast Mathematics, Ehad is reserved exclusively for Allah.

No created entity can possess Ehad.

Ehad is not part of the created mathematical universe.

Instead, it is the ultimate ontological reference upon which the existence of every created system depends.

Ehad never participates as a member of any Vahid.

Ehad has no multiplicity.

Ehad is unique by necessity.

---

# Primitive Object 2

# Yek

Yek is the primitive mathematical object representing the unique ontological identity of a created entity.

Every created entity possesses exactly one Yek.

No two created entities possess the same Yek.

Yek cannot be derived from any other mathematical object.

It is accepted as primitive.

The identity represented by a Yek remains unique regardless of scale.

A particle,

an atom,

a molecule,

a cell,

an organism,

or a civilization

may each possess its own Yek according to their ontological level.

---

# Primitive Object 3

# Vahid

Vahid is the organized ontological whole formed by Yek.

Vahid is not merely a mathematical set.

A Vahid represents an organized ontological structure whose members belong to the same ontological level and the same essential nature.

Every fundamental Yek belongs to exactly one fundamental Vahid.

Relations such as family,

city,

institution,

profession,

or temporary organization

do not define a new fundamental Vahid.

Instead, they are organizational structures occurring within an existing Vahid.

Vahid therefore represents ontological unity rather than arbitrary collection.

---

# Primitive Object 4

# Hebûn

Hebûn is the fundamental organizational principle of Zanistarast.

Hebûn is not an object.

Hebûn is not a material entity.

Hebûn is not merely a relation.

Hebûn is the principle by which the ontological layers constituting a Yek become an organized and coherent whole.

Hebûn preserves integrity,

organization,

continuity,

and internal harmony.

The same Hebûn principle recursively appears at every ontological scale.

Therefore,

atoms,

molecules,

cells,

organisms,

families,

societies,

and higher structures

may all be understood as manifestations of the same organizational principle.

---

# Primitive Object 5

# Zanabûn

Zanabûn is the primitive principle of organized knowledge.

Knowledge cannot exist independently of Hebûn.

Only an organized ontological structure can possess meaningful knowledge.

Therefore, Zanabûn always operates upon Hebûn.

Zanabûn transforms organized existence into organized knowledge.

---

# Primitive Object 6

# Mabûn

Mabûn is the primitive principle of persistent and validated memory.

Memory is not merely stored information.

Within Zanistarast Mathematics,

memory represents organized and verified continuity.

Only validated knowledge may become Mabûn.

Therefore,

Mabûn depends upon Zanabûn.

---

# Primitive Object 7

# Rabûn

Rabûn is the primitive principle of organized governance.

Governance does not merely represent authority.

Rather,

Rabûn represents the maintenance,

continuity,

and ordered operation

of an organized ontological structure.

Rabûn depends upon Mabûn,

because sustainable organization requires persistent memory.

---

# Primitive Object 8

# Rasterast

Rasterast is the primitive principle of deterministic verification.

Every mathematical statement,

every ontological proposition,

every organizational model,

and every derived theorem

must ultimately pass Rasterast verification.

Rasterast represents the final verification layer of Zanistarast Mathematics.

Statements failing Rasterast verification cannot be accepted as completed mathematical results.

---

# Ontological Dependency

The primitive objects are connected through the following deterministic hierarchy.

Ehad

↓

Yek

↓

Vahid

↓

Hebûn

↓

Zanabûn

↓

Mabûn

↓

Rabûn

↓

Rasterast

This hierarchy represents dependency rather than temporal order.

Each higher principle requires the previous one as its ontological foundation.

---

# Scope

These primitive definitions constitute the mathematical vocabulary of Zanistarast.

Every future definition,

axiom,

lemma,

theorem,

proof,

simulation,

and formal verification

shall be expressed using these primitive objects.

No additional primitive object shall be introduced unless a logical inconsistency is formally demonstrated.
