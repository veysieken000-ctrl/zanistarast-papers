# Chapter 01 — Primitive Mathematical Definitions

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This chapter defines the primitive mathematical concepts of the Zanistarast framework.

Primitive concepts are intentionally undefined in terms of more fundamental concepts.
They serve as the foundation upon which all later definitions, axioms, theorems and proofs are built.

---

# Primitive Concepts

The primitive concepts of Book I are

- Vahid
- Yek
- Hebûn
- Zanabûn
- Mabûn
- Rabûn
- Rasterast

No additional primitive concepts shall be introduced in Version 1.0.

---

# Definition 1 — Vahid

Vahid represents the undivided whole.

Properties

- complete
- indivisible as a whole
- contains created uniqueness
- foundation of system unity

---

# Definition 2 — Yek

Yek represents the uniqueness of every created entity.

Every created entity possesses exactly one Yek.

Yek consists of

- identity
- context
- history
- relations
- temporal trace
- spatial trace

Yek is immutable.

Yek cannot be shared.

---

# Definition 3 — Hebûn

Hebûn represents validated existence.

Only entities satisfying Hebûn are admitted into the mathematical system.

Hebûn is the first verification layer.

---

# Definition 4 — Zanabûn

Zanabûn represents validated knowledge.

Knowledge cannot exist inside the framework before Hebûn.

---

# Definition 5 — Mabûn

Mabûn represents structured knowledge.

Knowledge becomes an internally consistent model.

---

# Definition 6 — Rabûn

Rabûn represents executable structured knowledge.

Models become actions.

---

# Definition 7 — Rasterast

Rasterast represents complete deterministic verification.

Rasterast verifies the entire chain

Hebûn

↓

Zanabûn

↓

Mabûn

↓

Rabûn

It is the highest verification layer of Version 1.

---

# Reserved Definition

Yek denotes the uniqueness of created entities.

Ehad is reserved exclusively for the Divine attribute and is intentionally excluded from mathematical formalization.

---

# Scope

These primitive definitions are assumed throughout the remainder of Book I.

Later chapters may build upon them but shall not redefine them.
