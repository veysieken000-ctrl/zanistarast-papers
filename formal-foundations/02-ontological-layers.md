# Ontological Layers

Version: 1.0

Status: Ontological Layer Model

---

# Purpose

This chapter defines the ontological layer architecture of Zanistarast.

Every created entity exists through multiple dependent ontological layers.

These layers do not exist independently.

Each higher layer depends upon the integrity of the lower layers.

The hierarchy represents ontological necessity.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Ontological layers are ordered.

No higher layer may exist without the lower layers required to support it.

Each layer contributes a distinct aspect of existence.

Together they constitute a single organized Yek.

---

# Layer Hierarchy

The ontological hierarchy is expressed as

Physical

↓

Biological

↓

Mental

↓

Spiritual

↓

Ethical

↓

Judgment

Each level depends upon every preceding level.

---

# Physical Layer

The Physical Layer establishes material existence.

It includes

• matter,

• energy,

• space,

• time,

• physical interaction.

Without the Physical Layer,

no created material structure can exist.

---

# Biological Layer

The Biological Layer organizes physical structures into living systems.

It governs

• life,

• growth,

• inheritance,

• biological regulation,

• species continuity.

Biology presupposes physical existence.

---

# Mental Layer

The Mental Layer governs information processing.

It includes

• perception,

• recognition,

• learning,

• pattern formation,

• reasoning based upon available knowledge.

Mental activity organizes information,

but does not itself establish ethical responsibility.

---

# Dependency Principle

The Physical Layer supports the Biological Layer.

The Biological Layer supports the Mental Layer.

No layer replaces the previous one.

Instead,

each extends the organizational capacity of existence.

---

# Spiritual Layer

The Spiritual Layer establishes the unified living existence that emerges from the integrated operation of the Physical, Biological, and Mental Layers.

It is not independent of the lower layers.

Rather,

it represents their organized unity within a single living Yek.

The Spiritual Layer therefore expresses the continuity of the living being as one coherent existence.

---

# Ethical Layer

The Ethical Layer introduces moral responsibility.

It governs

• free choice,

• responsibility,

• intention,

• accountability,

• moral evaluation.

Unlike the Mental Layer,

the Ethical Layer does not merely process information.

It evaluates possible actions and makes responsible choices.

Therefore,

ethical responsibility begins at this layer.

---

# Judgment Layer

The Judgment Layer establishes the capacity for final evaluation.

It governs

• truth,

• justice,

• accountability,

• final judgment,

• objective evaluation.

Without the Ethical Layer,

Judgment has no foundation.

Therefore,

Judgment represents the highest created ontological layer.

---

# Complete Dependency

The complete ontological dependency is

Physical

↓

Biological

↓

Mental

↓

Spiritual

↓

Ethical

↓

Judgment

Each layer presupposes every preceding layer.

No higher layer can exist independently.

---

# Recursive Principle

The ontological layer model is recursive.

Every organized Yek exhibits layered organization appropriate to its ontological level.

The complexity of the layers increases,

but the dependency principle remains unchanged.

Thus,

the same organizational architecture is preserved throughout creation.

---

# Relationship with the Deterministic Chain

The ontological layers describe the internal architecture of a created Yek.

The Deterministic Chain describes the dependency between the primitive principles of Zanistarast.

The two frameworks are complementary.

Together they describe both

• the internal constitution of existence,

and

• the mathematical organization of existence.

---

# Summary

Every created existence possesses an ordered ontological architecture.

The Physical Layer provides material existence.

The Biological Layer provides life.

The Mental Layer provides information processing.

The Spiritual Layer unifies living existence.

The Ethical Layer establishes moral responsibility.

The Judgment Layer establishes final evaluation.

Together these layers define the complete ontological architecture of a created being.
