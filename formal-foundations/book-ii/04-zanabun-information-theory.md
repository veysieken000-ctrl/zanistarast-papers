# Zanabûn Information Theory

Version: 2.0

Status: Advanced Information Mathematics

---

# Purpose

Zanabûn Information Theory establishes the deterministic mathematics of organized knowledge.

Knowledge is not treated merely as stored information.

Instead,

knowledge is understood as validated organization built upon Hebûn.

The objective is to establish formal operators governing the creation,

transformation,

verification,

and preservation of organized knowledge.

---

# Definition

Zanabûn is organized knowledge emerging from a valid Hebûn.

Knowledge cannot exist independently of organization.

Therefore,

every valid Zanabûn presupposes an existing Hebûn.

---

# Fundamental Principle

Every valid Zanabûn satisfies

• Organization

• Consistency

• Verifiability

• Continuity

• Deterministic Interpretation

Violation of any one property invalidates the knowledge structure.

---

# Knowledge Operator

Define the knowledge operator

ℤ

such that

ℤ(H)=K

where

H

is a valid Hebûn

and

K

is organized knowledge.

Knowledge is therefore generated only from organized existence.

---

# Knowledge Integrity

Let

I(K)

represent knowledge integrity.

A knowledge structure is valid only if

I(K)=True.

Integrity requires

• internal consistency,

• deterministic meaning,

• compatibility with Hebûn,

• successful Rasterast verification.

---

# Knowledge Transformation

Let

Θ

represent a knowledge transformation.

Then

Θ(K)=K'

provided that

• organizational integrity is preserved,

• meaning is preserved,

• deterministic consistency is preserved.

Knowledge may evolve,

but it may not become internally contradictory.

---

# Knowledge Composition

Independent knowledge structures

K₁,

K₂,

...,

Kₙ

may be composed into a higher organized knowledge system.

Composition is valid only if

all participating knowledge structures are mutually compatible.

Otherwise,

composition is undefined.

---

# Knowledge Closure

Every valid Zanabûn is closed under valid knowledge operations.

No operation may introduce

undefined,

contradictory,

or unverifiable knowledge.

---

# Knowledge Degradation

Let

Δ(K)

represent knowledge degradation.

Knowledge degradation occurs whenever one or more of the following conditions fail

• organizational consistency,

• semantic coherence,

• deterministic interpretation,

• Rasterast verification.

If Δ(K) exceeds the acceptable threshold,

the knowledge structure is no longer considered a valid Zanabûn.

---

# Knowledge Recovery

Define the recovery operator

ρ(K)

such that

ρ(K') = K

where K' is a degraded knowledge structure.

Recovery is valid only if

• Hebûn integrity is preserved,

• semantic consistency is restored,

• deterministic meaning is recovered,

• Rasterast verification succeeds.

Recovery restores knowledge without altering its ontological identity.

---

# Recursive Knowledge

Every organized knowledge structure may itself become an element of a higher organized knowledge structure.

Thus

K₁,

K₂,

...,

Kₙ

may generate

K*

provided compatibility and deterministic consistency are preserved.

Knowledge therefore scales recursively.

---

# Knowledge Invariants

The following properties remain invariant under every valid Zanabûn transformation.

• organizational integrity,

• semantic consistency,

• deterministic interpretation,

• compatibility with Hebûn,

• Rasterast verifiability.

Violation of any invariant invalidates the transformation.

---

# Fundamental Theorem of Zanabûn

Every valid Zanabûn satisfies simultaneously

• deterministic organization,

• semantic consistency,

• recursive scalability,

• knowledge closure,

• recoverability,

• Rasterast verification.

If any one of these properties is absent,

the structure is not a valid Zanabûn.
