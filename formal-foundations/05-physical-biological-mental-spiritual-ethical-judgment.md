# Physical, Biological, Mental, Spiritual, Ethical and Judgment Layers

Version: 1.0

Status: Layer Definitions

---

# Purpose

This chapter formally defines the six ontological layers introduced in the previous chapters.

Each layer possesses a unique ontological function.

None of the layers is sufficient by itself.

Only their deterministic organization constitutes a complete created being.

---

# Layer I

## Physical Layer

The Physical Layer is the foundation of material existence.

It governs

• matter,

• energy,

• space,

• time,

• motion,

• physical interaction.

Every created material structure begins at this layer.

The Physical Layer provides existence,

but not life.

---

## Mathematical Role

The Physical Layer defines the structural substrate upon which all higher layers depend.

Without physical organization,

no higher ontological layer can emerge.

---

# Layer II

## Biological Layer

The Biological Layer transforms organized physical structures into living systems.

It governs

• cellular organization,

• metabolism,

• reproduction,

• genetic continuity,

• biological regulation.

Life therefore presupposes physical existence.

---

## Mathematical Role

The Biological Layer introduces organized living processes.

It preserves the continuity of species while remaining dependent upon the Physical Layer.

---

# Layer III

## Mental Layer

The Mental Layer governs information processing.

It includes

• perception,

• memory acquisition,

• recognition,

• learning,

• reasoning,

• problem solving.

The Mental Layer operates upon available information.

It does not independently establish moral responsibility.

---

## Mathematical Role

The Mental Layer transforms biological activity into organized cognition.

It processes knowledge but does not determine moral judgment.

---

# Layer IV

## Spiritual Layer

The Spiritual Layer establishes the unified living existence that emerges from the deterministic organization of the Physical, Biological, and Mental Layers.

It is not an independent material component.

Rather,

it represents the organized unity of life within a single created Yek.

The Spiritual Layer gives continuity and coherence to the living being.

---

## Mathematical Role

The Spiritual Layer mathematically unifies the lower layers into one living existence.

It transforms organized biological cognition into a coherent living self.

Without this layer,

the lower layers remain organized processes,

but not one unified living being.

---

# Layer V

## Ethical Layer

The Ethical Layer introduces moral responsibility.

It governs

• free will,

• intention,

• responsibility,

• moral choice,

• accountability.

Unlike the Mental Layer,

which evaluates information,

the Ethical Layer evaluates actions.

Therefore,

ethical responsibility begins only at this level.

---

## Mathematical Role

The Ethical Layer transforms cognition into responsible action.

It is the first layer at which praise,

blame,

reward,

and responsibility become meaningful.

---

# Layer VI

## Judgment Layer

The Judgment Layer establishes objective evaluation.

It governs

• justice,

• truth,

• final accountability,

• judgment,

• objective moral evaluation.

This layer completes the created ontological architecture.

---

## Mathematical Role

The Judgment Layer evaluates the complete organization of the lower layers.

It determines whether the Ethical Layer has acted consistently with truth.

Thus,

Judgment completes the ontological architecture.

---

# Relationship with Hebûn

Hebûn organizes the entire layered architecture.

Without Hebûn,

the six layers cannot function as one organized existence.

Thus,

Hebûn is the organizational principle,

while the six layers constitute the organized structure.

---

# Summary

The six ontological layers define the complete architecture of a created human being.

The Physical Layer establishes material existence.

The Biological Layer establishes life.

The Mental Layer establishes cognition.

The Spiritual Layer establishes unified living existence.

The Ethical Layer establishes moral responsibility.

The Judgment Layer establishes objective accountability.

Together,

these six layers are organized by Hebûn,

constitute one Tek,

and participate in the complete deterministic architecture of Zanistarast.
